I. The opening

The promise, and the doubling plan.

Stelmakh & Associates has built its reputation on a specific promise: three professionals on every matter, a 48-hour response commitment, RFEs and NOIDs covered under the flat fee, and a founder who has been practicing business immigration since 2006. The firm has publicly stated the goal of doubling revenue and team year over year, and the current pipeline reflects that ambition, from Silicon Valley origins through Seattle headquarters to conference engagements at MWC Barcelona and eMerge Americas.

The question we would like to put to the firm is narrow and practical. What does the workflow look like one year into the doubling plan, and which steps should be handled by an orchestration layer before the next associate is hired.

II. The frame

60 / 30 / 10.

60%
Structured information handling

Evidence intake, indexing, cross-reference checks, document assembly, first-draft narrative against known USCIS criteria. Traditional code and database work with an assistant layer on top.

30%
Rule-based logic

Eligibility screening, criterion mapping, RFE-response pattern matching against the firm’s own library of prior successful responses. Codified knowledge, not generative guesswork.

10%
Attorney judgment

Strategy, framing the narrative, client counsel, argument in response to a difficult RFE. This layer does not belong to an agent. The firm’s competitive moat lives here.

Most firms evaluating AI tools treat the question as binary. Either adopt a platform or decline it. The more useful frame separates a case into three layers. The question we would bring to the firm is which parts of the 60 and 30 are currently carried by associate and paralegal time, and what it would cost to move them to the right layer without disturbing the 10 that matters.

III. The relevant case

VigilOre. Document-heavy, regulated, compressed.

A recent engagement with VigilOre, an extractive-industries compliance platform, compressed 160-plus hours of regulatory documentation work to under five minutes using a multi-agent orchestration layer built on top of the client’s existing document and data systems. The parallel to O-1A and EB-1A petition assembly is direct: a dense and varied evidentiary record, a known regulatory framework, and a narrative product at the end.

The goal in a Stelmakh engagement would not be a 2,000x compression. A three to five times improvement in first-draft time, holding attorney review time constant, is sufficient to absorb the volume growth the firm has publicly committed to.

“A reduction in drafting time on an RFE response flows straight to margin, because the firm already covers those responses at no added cost.”

IV. The relevant paper

Interpretable Context Methodology.

Interpretable Context Methodology, submitted to ACM Transactions on Interactive Intelligent Systems, sets out how agent context can be organized as a layered filesystem with measurable interpretability and reproducibility. For a firm where the product is attorney craft and the buyer is a client trusting that craft, an interpretable and auditable drafting layer is the only version of this that is worth deploying.
